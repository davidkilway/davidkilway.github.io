About

My first real exposure to optics was through my dad's old med school microscopes. As a kid, I spent hours looking at whatever I could find and would culture paramecium, hunt for tardigrades, and look for rotifers in bird bath water. Around the same time, my brother got really into astronomy which pulled me in that direction as well. Optics ended up being the subject connecting both.

I initially came to Rochester as a Biomedical Engineering major, but switched to Optical Engineering after meeting people in the department. The people and the work pulled me in immediately and I haven't looked back since.
