About

I study at the crossroads of physics, biology, and engineering — not because I couldn't pick one, but because the most interesting problems tend to sit between disciplines.

Right now I'm focused on low-cost optical instruments: things that can do real science without requiring a fully equipped lab. I'm interested in what happens when you bring that kind of tooling into biological contexts.

I work mostly in Python and CAD, and I'm comfortable at a lab bench. I build things to understand them, and I document the parts that didn't work as much as the parts that did.
